Literature Findings:

  • Incorporating Time-Based Data into Web Maps

    Most web maps present data as static snapshots, typically representing a single moment in time. However, spatial data often changes depending on the time of day, week, or season. Introducing a temporal dimension into mapping platforms would allow users to explore how conditions vary over time for instance, tracking the fluctuating footfall of public spaces throughout the day. This temporal visualisation could be particularly useful for infrastructure planning, such as determining optimal times for road closures or identifying underused areas. Presenting this data as an animated timeline directly on the map could be more intuitive and accessible than traditional graphs (e.g., the bar graphs used in Google Maps’ "Popular Times" feature).

  • Mapping Local Suppliers and Construction Workers

    Another important dataset to integrate involves local suppliers and construction workers. Projects in the built environment often rely heavily on locally sourced materials and labor, which need to be identified, evaluated, and engaged quickly and reliably. A web mapping platform that visualizes the geographic distribution and availability of these resources would significantly support early-stage project planning and supply chain resilience.

  • Capturing Views and Popularity Through Photographs

    The visual appeal and photographic representation of a location play a key role in its popularity, walkability, and perceived livability. These visual cues could be leveraged by harvesting data from social media platforms. Mapping the locations with the highest density of geotagged photos or user posts could reveal informal "hotspots" that are culturally or socially significant, yet overlooked in formal planning processes.

  • Facilitating User Interaction and Participatory Feedback

    One limitation of current web mapping platforms such as Google Maps is the minimal interaction between users and the map beyond navigation. A more participatory model could incorporate community feedback tools, enabling users to contribute comments, ideas, or even vote on proposed developments. This would shift the platform from a one-way display tool to a two-way communication channel. For example, in a student-dominated neighbourhood, users might suggest more leisure amenities like cinemas or cafés. Implementing a structured comment and voting system, with safeguards to reduce bias and manipulation, could help planners and architects better understand and prioritise community needs.

  • Waste & Resource Tracking

Visualise local recycling points, construction waste collection areas, or reuse centres. Especially useful for sustainable architecture or circular economy approaches.

  • Public Transport Accessibility & Emissions Reduction

    Map areas with high public transport coverage and compare against car dependency zones. Suggest sustainable transport enhancements or walkability improvements.

1. Code & Structure Improvements

  • Splitting into smaller functions/modules improves readability and maintainability. For example:
    • addSources()
    • addLayers()
    • setupPopups()
    • setupToggleHandlers()
    • fetchAndAddOverpassData()
  • Debounce toggle handlers if needed: If users toggle rapidly, debounce can prevent excessive redraws.
  • Use constants and enums: Your layer IDs and colors are repeated. Group these in objects or constants at the top.
  • Async/await: Use async functions for fetch calls to make the code cleaner.
  • Map style parameterization: If you want different base maps or styles, make the style URL configurable.

2. UI/UX Improvements

  • Layer legend: Add a legend to explain what colors and symbols mean, helping users quickly understand the map.
  • Improved toggle UI: Use nicer toggle switches or multi-select dropdowns with searchable options if you expect many layers.
  • Better cluster interaction: Show aggregated info about clustered points on click (e.g., number of trees, species distribution).
  • Highlight interactions: Add a hover effect on features, e.g., outline buildings when hovered, highlight paths.
  • Details panel: On clicking a feature, show a side panel with rich info, images, documents, or related data instead of just a popup.
  • Mobile support: The toggle controls and map need to be tested and optimized for smaller screens.

3. Functional & Visualization Enhancements

  • Impact analysis layers:
    • Add shadow analysis or sun path visualizations for buildings (important for urban designers).
    • Show noise contours or pollution data if you can access them.
    • Overlay demographics or census data to indicate community needs.
  • 3D model integration:
    • Integrate with tools like Cesium or Three.js for detailed 3D modeling of proposed developments.
    • Allow uploading and visualizing proposed building footprints and heights to compare with existing context.
  • Dynamic analytics:
    • Provide metrics on area coverage, green space ratio, walkability score, etc.
    • Enable what-if scenarios where users can “draw” or “upload” new developments and see how it changes the map statistics.
  • Collaboration features:
    • Allow multiple users to annotate or comment on areas.
    • Versioning of proposed plans.
  • Real-time updates:
    • If feasible, integrate live feeds such as construction status, traffic flows, or planning applications.

4. Data Enhancements

  • Data completeness:
    • Your POIs come from OSM — consider integrating local council datasets for planning permissions, development constraints, protected areas, or utility locations.
    • Bring in land ownership boundaries, flood risk zones, and heritage overlays.
  • Attribute enrichment:
    • When showing buildings, include use type, year built, construction materials if available.
    • For trees, show species, age, health status (important for landscape architects).

5. Visual Improvements & Accessibility

  • Improved color palettes:
    • Use colorblind-friendly palettes (ColorBrewer or similar).
    • Adjust opacity or use patterns for overlapping layers.
  • Legend interactivity:
    • Clicking on a legend item could toggle that layer’s visibility.
  • Accessibility:
    • Ensure contrast and text sizes are readable.
    • Make sure keyboard navigation works for toggles.
